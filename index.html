What if we chose differently?

Governments hold the budgets, but the public holds the governments. Every figure you have just seen is the result of choices, and choices can change. The money to solve our greatest crises already exists inside one year of military spending. This is not a question of capacity. It is a question of will.

What if this was your back garden? Your family in the rubble, your children out of school? We share one planet and one future. So take the $2.89 trillion, and decide.

⚠ Important disclaimer
This is a work of data art and advocacy, not a policy document. The reality of global defence, conflict, and humanitarian funding is vastly more nuanced than any single visualisation can capture. Defence spending serves multiple purposes: deterrence, disaster response, peacekeeping, and geopolitical stability, and many military personnel serve with genuine dedication and sacrifice. The cost figures for solving global issues are estimates derived from peer-reviewed and UN agency sources, representing additional investment gaps above current spending. Many of these issues are deeply interrelated and cannot be solved by money alone: governance, political will, institutional capacity, and cultural change are equally critical.

This visualisation is intended to provoke reflection on the scale of global resource allocation, not to offer simple solutions to complex problems. We have not invented or inflated any figure; all data was verified against primary sources in June 2026. We acknowledge the immense complexity faced by policymakers navigating genuine security threats.

The world is not simple. But some of its choices are starker than we allow ourselves to see.
